The Manifold of Completed Intelligibility · Essay 5 of 10

Neighboring Sentences, Different Survivors

How small linguistic differences disclose distinct forms of completed intelligibility

Series movement: Essay 4 established the recovery method. This essay tests discrimination among neighboring sentence forms. Essay 6 will move from local settlements to theorem traversal.

1. The strongest tests are often the closest cases

A classification system can appear impressive when applied only to radically different examples. It is easy to distinguish a command from a proof, a question from a definition, or a legal ruling from a narrative description. The harder test is whether the method can separate sentences that share almost everything visible.

Neighboring cases matter because they reduce irrelevant variation. The subject may remain constant. The vocabulary may differ by one word. The external situation may be the same. What changes is the way the intelligibility becomes complete.

The method is strongest when it can show that a small surface difference corresponds to an exact structural difference in X-pressure, answering work, topology, or survivor.

This essay therefore examines sentence pairs and groups in which one dimension changes while others remain stable. The aim is not to multiply examples for their own sake. It is to show how the field discriminates.

2. The door pair: Does and Is

The door closed.
The door is closed.

The pair remains the cleanest introductory contrast.

In the first sentence, the live pressure is an incomplete path. The answering movement carries the closing event to its endpoint. The result survives as an accomplishment.

In the second sentence, the live pressure is an unresolved present alternative. The answering movement settles which condition governs. The result survives as a standing.

Dimension The door closed. The door is closed.
X-pressure An unfinished closing transition. An unsettled present condition.
Answering movement Arrival at the terminal condition. Settlement of the governing alternative.
Completed Y The door has completed closing. The door stands closed.
Work-family Rest Rest
Orientation Toward Toward
Survivor-mode Does Is

3. A standing and a standing-with-bearing

The value is ten.
The value is ten under the stated condition.

At first glance, both sentences seem to preserve the same result. Yet the second does not leave the value standing alone. Its intelligibility remains bound to a condition.

For the first sentence, Y is simply the settled value. What survives is the result itself.

For the second, Y is a conditional settlement. What survives is the value together with the condition under which it holds.

Sentence Survivor Likely mode
The value is ten. The settled numerical result. Is
The value is ten under the stated condition. The result together with its holding condition. With

The addition of the condition does not merely add content. It changes the form of persistence. The result is no longer fully recoverable apart from the bearing that qualifies it.

4. Toward and Across: focal result versus constitutive plurality

The first case establishes the rule.
The rule holds across all five cases.

The first sentence gathers completion around one focal case. The second completes only by holding a plurality together.

In the first, the relevant landing is centered on the first case as a governing return point. In the second, the five-case range is constitutive of the settlement. Remove the plurality and the completion changes.

Dimension First case establishes the rule Rule holds across all five cases
Topology Focal Distributed
Orientation Toward Across
What completes Y Return to one decisive case Holding the full case range together

The important point is that orientation concerns the topology of completion, not the surface presence of singular or plural nouns. A sentence may mention many objects yet settle Toward one governing centre. Another may mention one collective noun yet settle Across a constitutive plurality.

5. Away: completion through release

A second route is available.
The route is no longer compulsory.

Both sentences concern alternatives to a path, but they complete differently.

“A second route is available” expands the field by adding another traversal. Its work is Expansion.

“The route is no longer compulsory” settles through release from prior governance. The critical event is not merely addition but defeat of compulsion.

This contrast shows why Away cannot be reduced to negative wording. The second sentence is structurally Away because Y becomes complete through release from the former governor.

6. Distinction and Relation: difference versus bearing

A is distinct from B.
A depends on B.

Both sentences involve two terms, but the work differs.

The first installs or stabilizes a difference. Its answering movement makes non-identity operative.

The second establishes a dependency while preserving both terms as independently role-capable.

Sentence What X lacks What movement supplies Work-family
A is distinct from B. An operative difference criterion. Separation or non-identity. Distinction
A depends on B. A specified bearing. Dependency. Relation

The presence of two terms does not determine Relation. If the work is to separate, the family is Distinction. If the work is to connect, it is Relation.

7. Correction and Integration: repair versus organization

The beam is moved inward to restore balance.
The beams are joined to form a stable frame.

Both sentences may concern stability, but their work differs.

In the first, a configuration violates a criterion. The answering movement changes an offending parameter until admissibility is restored. This is Correction.

In the second, several parts lack a whole-dependent organization. The answering movement assigns them roles within a frame. This is Integration.

This distinction is especially important in mathematical and technical writing, where repair and construction often appear together. One sentence may correct a value; the next may integrate corrected parts into a system.

8. Relation and Integration: connection versus whole-dependent role

The two variables correspond.
The two variables form one coordinate system.

The first sentence establishes a bearing. The terms remain independently role-capable.

The second assigns the variables roles in a whole whose capacity depends on their organization.

Relation connects. Integration reorganizes.

Feature Correspondence Coordinate system
Terms retain independent roles Yes Only within a larger organization
Whole-dependent capacity emerges Not required Yes
Work-family Relation Integration

9. Rest and Correction: closure versus repair

The proof is corrected.
The proof is complete.

The first sentence answers a misfit. The second answers a continuation demand.

“The proof is corrected” presupposes a criterion violation and a repair.

“The proof is complete” presupposes an unresolved question as to whether further steps are required and discharges that question.

The resulting states may coexist, but the sentences perform different work.

Completion vocabulary does not guarantee Rest

A sentence saying that something is “complete” may describe a standing inherited from prior work rather than perform the terminal discharge itself. Context remains decisive.

10. A legal contrast: standing, condition, and effect

The licence is valid.
The licence is valid while the fee remains paid.
The licence authorizes entry.

These three sentences illustrate the survivor modes.

The first preserves a standing result: validity.

The second preserves validity together with a constitutive condition.

The third preserves an operative legal capacity: authorization.

Sentence What survives Mode
The licence is valid. A legal standing. Is
The licence is valid while the fee remains paid. The standing with its condition. With
The licence authorizes entry. An operative legal power. Does

Legal interpretation frequently turns on exactly these differences. A status, a status-with-condition, and a power are not interchangeable survivors.

11. A scientific contrast: observation, relation, and law

The sample expanded.
The expansion increased with temperature.
The material expands when heated.

The first sentence preserves an accomplished event.

The second establishes a dependency between two quantities.

The third preserves a repeatable capacity or law-like disposition.

The topic remains expansion, but the completed intelligibility changes from event, to relation, to generalized operation.

This is one reason scientific language benefits from survivor analysis. Observation reports, correlations, mechanisms, and laws may share vocabulary while leaving different forms of inheritance.

12. Mathematical neighboring forms

The point is marked.
The point is marked relative to the origin.
The mark excludes points outside the boundary.

The first leaves a marked standing.

The second leaves the mark together with a constitutive frame of reference.

The third leaves an operative exclusion effect.

In a mathematical derivation, such differences can determine later availability. A standing can be cited. A frame-bound result must be inherited with its reference structure. An operative effect constrains what later steps may admit.

The full corpus allows these distinctions to be examined repeatedly under changing mathematical content.

13. Why the same sentence can require different recovery in different contexts

Structural recovery is not performed on isolated wording alone.

Consider:

The boundary is fixed.

In one context, the sentence may settle which boundary governs: Rest / Toward / Is.

In another, it may report the result of a correction: the defective limit has been reset.

In another, it may establish a distinction by installing an operative limit.

The wording is identical, but inherited X-pressure differs.

This does not make the method arbitrary. It shows that completed intelligibility belongs to the sentence in context rather than to the string abstracted from every use.

The coordinate belongs to the settlement carried by the sentence, not to the words considered apart from their inherited opening.

14. Designing stronger minimal-pair tests

A useful neighboring test should hold as much as possible constant while changing one structural feature.

Good test pairs may preserve:

  • the same subject;
  • the same broad result;
  • the same tense;
  • the same lexical field;
  • or the same external situation.

They may vary:

  • event versus state;
  • standing versus condition-bearing standing;
  • focal versus distributed completion;
  • addition versus release;
  • relation versus integration;
  • repair versus closure;
  • or result versus operative effect.

Such pairs can test whether analysts recover the intended structural difference independently.

15. When neighboring cases should remain unresolved

Not every close pair admits immediate placement.

Some sentences are deliberately ambiguous. Others inherit too little context. Some contain several nested events. Some translations erase aspectual distinctions. In such cases, the method should withhold.

Withholding is especially important in neighboring analysis because the temptation to force a contrast is strong. The fact that two sentences differ grammatically does not guarantee that they differ in coordinate. Conversely, identical wording in different contexts may carry different settlements.

The correct question is not:

How can these two sentences be made different?

It is:

Does positive recovery show that they complete differently?

16. From crafted contrasts to corpus evidence

Minimal pairs teach the dimensions clearly, but they remain controlled examples.

The 615-theorem corpus provides the stronger environment. There, neighboring distinctions arise under actual mathematical pressure rather than being designed for illustration.

One theorem sentence may establish a direct relation. The next may integrate that relation into a larger whole. A terminal sentence may convert a proof path into a portable result. Similar wording may recur under different inherited openings across the eleven movements.

Corpus analysis can therefore ask:

  • Do neighboring coordinates remain distinguishable under technical abstraction?
  • Do independent analysts recover the same differences?
  • Do local distinctions predict later proof dependencies?
  • Do changes in survivor-mode correspond to changes in how later theorems inherit the result?

The strongest evidence will come when coordinate distinctions explain why later steps can or cannot proceed.

17. What neighboring analysis reveals

Neighboring sentence analysis establishes several principles.

  1. Surface similarity does not guarantee structural identity.
  2. Shared topic does not determine work-family.
  3. Grammatical form does not by itself determine survivor-mode.
  4. Context can change X without changing wording.
  5. Small changes can alter topology or persistence.
  6. The coordinate is recovered from the whole settlement path.

Most importantly, the method becomes discriminative rather than merely descriptive. It can identify where a difference lies.

18. The next scale: from sentence pairs to proof traversal

So far, the series has treated local settlements one at a time.

But mathematical proof does not consist of isolated local landings. Each completed sentence leaves something inherited by the next. A relation established in one sentence may become the X-condition of an integration. A corrected configuration may become the premise of a terminal Rest. A theorem conclusion may preserve both a result and a route.

The next essay will therefore move from coordinate discrimination to composition.

Its central question will be:

What makes a theorem more than a list of completed sentences?

19. Structural difference at close range

The fifty-four-form field becomes credible only if it can distinguish close cases without depending on impression.

The examples in this essay show how neighboring sentences can vary in:

  • the pressure they answer;
  • the work they perform;
  • the topology through which they settle;
  • and the mode in which they survive.

The door pair changes Does to Is. The conditional-value pair changes Is to With. The focal and distributed examples change Toward to Across. The route pair distinguishes expansion from release. The beam pair distinguishes correction from integration.

These are not merely differences of phrasing. They are differences in completed intelligibility.

A sentence is structurally different from another when the path by which it lands, or the form in which its landing survives, is different.

The next task is to show how such local differences compose into a proof.
