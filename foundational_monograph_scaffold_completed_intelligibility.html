Abstract

Abstract Seeded

Seed text: This monograph presents a systematic ontology of completed intelligibility. It proposes that every properly individuated completed intelligibility lands through one of fifty-four pairwise discrete event-forms generated by six work-families, three settlement orientations, and three survivor-modes. It develops a multilingual recovery method, distinguishes local settlements from traversals, and examines the theory through a dependency-ordered mathematical corpus of 615 theorems across eleven movements. The work argues that language realizes rather than exhausts a deeper field of completion, and that this field operates historically, intersubjectively, constitutively, anonymously, and generatively.
